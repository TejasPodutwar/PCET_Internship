Curriculum Overview
The core curriculum of Civil Engineering introduces principles of:
  • Geo-technical/Foundation Engineering
  • Water Resources Engineering
  • Environmental Engineering
  • Construction Engineering
These principles provides a grounding in the fundamentals of core areas of Civil Engineering, and incorporates project-based learning that teach the processes and skills involved in planning, design and construction. The curriculum provides students with both a rigorous foundation in theory and practicals, hands-on experience and also site experience which they need to succeed in the field.
The programme comprises of 8 Semesters. An academic year is divided into two terms â€“ I and II. Term I shall have Semester I, III, V and VII whereas Term II shall have Semester II, IV, VI and VIII. In each semester, courses are offered in 12 to 14 teaching weeks and the remaining 5 weeks are to be utilized for conduct of examinations and evaluation purposes.
For the students, each week has 40 working hours that includes 5/6 working days a week, consisting of lectures, tutorials, assignments, activities, class participation, library work, special counseling, sports, project work, field visit, student welfare and social activities. Every course offered have Lecture, Practical/Tutorial and site visits associated with the teaching-learning process.
